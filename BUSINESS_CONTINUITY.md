# MIA Enterprise AGI - Business Continuity Plan

## Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

This document outlines the business continuity and disaster recovery procedures for MIA Enterprise AGI.

### 1. Business Continuity Overview

#### 1.1 Objectives
- Ensure continuous operation of critical business functions
- Minimize downtime and service disruption
- Protect data integrity and availability
- Maintain compliance with regulatory requirements

#### 1.2 Scope
- All MIA Enterprise AGI systems and services
- Critical business processes and functions
- Data backup and recovery procedures
- Emergency response and communication

### 2. Business Impact Analysis

#### 2.1 Critical Business Functions
- **Core AI Processing**: Primary intelligence and reasoning capabilities
- **Memory Systems**: Data storage and retrieval functions
- **Security Systems**: Authentication and access control
- **User Interface**: System interaction and communication

#### 2.2 Impact Assessment
- **Financial Impact**: Revenue loss and recovery costs
- **Operational Impact**: Service disruption and productivity loss
- **Regulatory Impact**: Compliance violations and penalties
- **Reputational Impact**: Customer confidence and brand damage

### 3. Recovery Objectives

#### 3.1 Recovery Time Objectives (RTO)
- **Critical Systems**: 1 hour maximum downtime
- **Important Systems**: 4 hours maximum downtime
- **Standard Systems**: 24 hours maximum downtime
- **Non-Critical Systems**: 72 hours maximum downtime

#### 3.2 Recovery Point Objectives (RPO)
- **Critical Data**: 15 minutes maximum data loss
- **Important Data**: 1 hour maximum data loss
- **Standard Data**: 4 hours maximum data loss
- **Non-Critical Data**: 24 hours maximum data loss

### 4. Risk Assessment

#### 4.1 Threat Categories
- **Natural Disasters**: Fire, flood, earthquake, severe weather
- **Technology Failures**: Hardware failure, software corruption, network outage
- **Human Factors**: Human error, sabotage, key personnel loss
- **External Threats**: Cyber attacks, supply chain disruption, utility failure

#### 4.2 Risk Mitigation
- Redundant systems and infrastructure
- Regular backup and testing procedures
- Emergency response training
- Vendor and supplier diversification

### 5. Backup and Recovery Procedures

#### 5.1 Data Backup Strategy
- **Full Backups**: Weekly complete system backup
- **Incremental Backups**: Daily incremental changes
- **Real-time Replication**: Critical data continuous replication
- **Offsite Storage**: Secure remote backup storage

#### 5.2 Recovery Procedures
1. **Assessment**: Evaluate extent of disruption
2. **Activation**: Activate appropriate recovery procedures
3. **Recovery**: Restore systems and data from backups
4. **Validation**: Verify system integrity and functionality
5. **Communication**: Notify stakeholders of recovery status

### 6. Emergency Response

#### 6.1 Emergency Response Team
- **Business Continuity Manager**: Overall coordination
- **IT Recovery Team**: Technical system recovery
- **Communications Team**: Stakeholder communication
- **Facilities Team**: Physical infrastructure support

#### 6.2 Response Procedures
1. **Immediate Response**: Ensure personnel safety
2. **Damage Assessment**: Evaluate impact and scope
3. **Decision Making**: Determine recovery strategy
4. **Implementation**: Execute recovery procedures
5. **Monitoring**: Track recovery progress

### 7. Alternative Processing Sites

#### 7.1 Hot Site
- Fully equipped backup facility
- Real-time data replication
- Immediate failover capability
- 24/7 availability

#### 7.2 Cold Site
- Basic infrastructure and utilities
- Equipment installation required
- Longer recovery time
- Cost-effective option

#### 7.3 Cloud-Based Recovery
- Virtual infrastructure in cloud
- Scalable resources on demand
- Geographic distribution
- Rapid deployment capability

### 8. Communication Plan

#### 8.1 Internal Communication
- Emergency notification system
- Regular status updates
- Escalation procedures
- Decision-making authority

#### 8.2 External Communication
- Customer notification procedures
- Vendor and supplier communication
- Regulatory reporting requirements
- Media relations protocol

### 9. Testing and Maintenance

#### 9.1 Testing Schedule
- **Quarterly**: Backup restoration testing
- **Semi-Annual**: Partial recovery testing
- **Annual**: Full disaster recovery exercise
- **Ad-hoc**: Component and procedure testing

#### 9.2 Test Procedures
1. **Planning**: Define test objectives and scope
2. **Execution**: Conduct test according to procedures
3. **Evaluation**: Assess test results and performance
4. **Documentation**: Record findings and recommendations
5. **Improvement**: Update procedures based on results
